# Problems with Existing Systems

Existing trust technologies solve important problems within their domains. PTI does not replace them — it orchestrates and composes their outputs. The problems below are **structural**, not product-specific.

## Fragmentation

| Problem | Consequence |
|---------|-------------|
| Per-institution trust stacks | Months of integration per vertical |
| No portable subject identity | Citizens re-prove at every onboarding |
| Context-blind scoring | Lending signals applied to rental decisions |
| Opaque score changes | No machine-readable provenance |

## Exclusion

Traditional bureau models exclude **thin-file** populations — those with little formal credit history but substantial real-world trust signals (community validation, merchant activity, employment history).

## Governance vacuum

Most trust systems lack:
- Consent-first signal ingestion
- Tenant-scoped data boundaries
- Auditable decision replay
- Cross-institution interoperability contracts

## Composability failure

Identity providers, KYC vendors, fraud engines, and credit bureaus each produce valuable signals — but no infrastructure layer composes them into context-scoped, explainable trust intelligence.
