# Trademark and Branding

**Portable Trust Infrastructure** and **PTI** identify a public specification category and ecosystem. Trademarks protect subjects and buyers from misleading compatibility claims while preserving open implementation rights.

PTI marks are **not** owned by any single implementer. During Phase 1–3, trademark administration is performed by the stewardship organization; Phase 4 targets transfer to an independent foundation ([Future Foundation Model](./future-foundation-model)).

## Protected marks

| Mark | Typical use |
|------|-------------|
| **Portable Trust Infrastructure™** | Category name in formal documents |
| **PTI™** | Short form in technical and marketing contexts |
| **PTI Compatible™** | Accredited certification mark (restricted) |
| **PTI Certified™** | Accredited certification mark (restricted) |
| PTI logo | Visual identity on certificates and official materials |

Exact legal status and registration jurisdictions are published in stewardship trademark notices. This document defines **policy intent**.

## Permitted use without license

The following **MAY** be used fairly and accurately:

- "Implements Portable Trust Infrastructure RFCs" in technical documentation
- "PTI-compatible Core profile v1.0 (self-assessed)" with accurate scope
- Academic papers citing PTI as a specification category
- Nominative reference to compare products ("our platform implements PTI lookup tiers")

Nominative use **MUST** be truthful and **MUST NOT** imply endorsement by the Working Group or stewardship organization.

## Requires certification or explicit permission

| Use | Requirement |
|-----|-------------|
| **PTI Certified** / **PTI Compatible** logos on product pages | Valid [certificate](./certification-process) |
| "Official PTI implementation" | **Prohibited** — no exclusive official vendor |
| PTI logo on merchandise or investor decks | Written trademark license |
| Modified PTI logos | **Prohibited** without approval |

Self-assessed implementations **MUST NOT** use certification marks. Acceptable phrasing: *"Self-assessed against PTI Core v1.0 tests; not independently certified."*

## Implementer branding

Implementers **MAY** brand their products independently:

| Acceptable | Unacceptable |
|------------|--------------|
| "Acme Trust Exchange — PTI Core certified" | "Acme PTI" implying Acme owns PTI |
| "Built on open PTI RFCs" | "The PTI Platform by Acme" |
| TumiTrust product name alongside accurate PTI claims | "PTI powered exclusively by TumiTrust" |

## Working Group and RFC attribution

RFC and governance documents **SHOULD** credit authors by name. Organizational logos on normative RFC pages **MUST NOT** imply sole ownership of the standard.

## Misrepresentation

The stewardship **MAY** pursue trademark remedies for:

- Certified claims without valid certificate
- "PTI compliant" when required tests fail
- Subject-facing materials suggesting PTI guarantees approval for credit or services
- Domain squatting or phishing using PTI marks

Good-faith errors **SHOULD** receive notice and cure period before enforcement.

## Co-branding with TumiTrust

TumiTrust **MAY** describe itself as:

- Founding steward and reference implementation
- Contributor to the PTI Working Group
- A certified implementer (when applicable)

TumiTrust **MUST NOT** claim exclusive rights to PTI or require partners to imply PTI equals TumiTrust.

## Transition to foundation

Upon foundation formation, trademark portfolio **SHOULD** transfer with:

- Royalty-free specification rights unchanged
- Certification mark rules preserved
- Public trademark guidelines updated within 90 days
